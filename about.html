About me

As an engineering student, I am naturally drawn to extracurricular pursuits, and one of my passions is photography. While I have always been captivated by DSLR cameras, I embarked on a journey to curate a gallery exclusively featuring images captured by my humble mobile phone. Thus, I began capturing moments, infusing them with essential elements of aesthetics and illusion, and sharing the fruits of my labor across various social media platforms. The concept of 'Object-Oriented Photography' has become the central focus of my lens, and I am continually enriching my skills by drawing knowledge from a diverse array of sources. I am confident that I will consistently produce even more captivating content in the future.

Padmanabha Roy


Photography has undergone a profound transformation in the context of the modern social world. It has evolved into a universal language, shaping the way we communicate, express emotions, and perceive our surroundings. The advent of smartphones has made photography incredibly accessible, allowing people from all walks of life to actively participate in visual storytelling. Social media platforms like Instagram and Snapchat have become virtual galleries where individuals share their lives, creativity, and perspectives with a global audience, transcending geographical boundaries. Moreover, photography has emerged as a potent tool for advocacy and social change. Iconic images can shed light on global injustices, provoke movements, and alter public perceptions of critical issues. However, this digital revolution in photography also raises ethical and authenticity concerns. Filters and editing tools can distort reality, blurring the lines between truth and fiction. Therefore, photography's role in the modern social world is multifaceted. It empowers individuals, connects communities, and drives societal change, all while prompting us to consider the ethical implications of our visual narratives in the digital age.
